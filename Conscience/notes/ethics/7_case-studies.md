# Case Studies
## Case Study 1: AI Hiring System Rejects Non‑Standard CVs
A company uses an AI system to filter job applications. The model rejects CVs that do not follow a standard format, unintentionally disadvantaging applicants from non‑Western backgrounds or people with disabilities who use assistive formatting tools.

The company discovers the issue. Fixing it will take time and money.

### Ethical Analysis

- **Principlism**: The company has a duty to ensure fairness and non‑discrimination in hiring. They should prioritize fixing the issue to uphold these principles, even if it is costly.
- **Consequentialism**: The company should weigh the consequences of fixing the issue against the harm caused by continuing to reject qualified candidates. The long‑term benefits of a more inclusive hiring process likely outweigh the short‑term costs.
- **Virtue Ethics**: A virtuous company would act with integrity and compassion, striving to create an inclusive hiring process that values diversity and treats all applicants with respect.

### Comparison of Frameworks

| Framework | Outcome |
| --- | --- |
| Principlism | Stop using the system immediately |
| Consequentialism | Continue with safeguards while fixing |
| Virtue Ethics | Pause system + transparent communication |

## Case Study 2: Smart Home Device Collects More Data Than Necessary
A smart home assistant collects voice data to improve speech recognition. Engineers discover it also records background conversations unintentionally. Fixing the issue will reduce model accuracy.

### Ethical Analysis

- **Principlism**: The company has a duty to respect user privacy. They should prioritize fixing the issue to uphold this principle, even if it reduces accuracy.
- **Consequentialism**: The company should weigh the benefits of improved accuracy against the harm caused by privacy violations. The long‑term trust of users likely outweighs the short‑term benefits of better performance.
- **Virtue Ethics**: A virtuous company would act with honesty and respect for users, prioritizing their privacy and well‑being over technical performance.

### Comparison of Frameworks

| Framework | Outcome |
| --- | --- |
| Principlism | Stop immediately |
| Consequentialism | Continue with safeguards + transparency |
| Virtue Ethics | Stop + communicate openly |

## Case Study 3: Developer Discovers a Security Vulnerability
A software developer finds a major security flaw in a widely used open‑source library.
Reporting it publicly could help attackers exploit it before a patch is ready.
Keeping it secret risks leaving millions of users vulnerable.

### Ethical Analysis

- **Principlism**: The developer has a duty to protect users from harm. They should report the vulnerability to the maintainers immediately, even if it risks exploitation.
- **Consequentialism**: The developer should weigh the potential harm of exploitation against the benefits of a quick fix. Coordinating with the maintainers to release a patch before public disclosure may minimize harm.
- **Virtue Ethics**: A virtuous developer would act with responsibility and integrity, prioritizing the safety of users while working transparently with the maintainers to address the issue.

### Comparison of Frameworks

| Framework | Outcome |
| --- | --- |
| Principlism | Private report only |
| Consequentialism | Coordinated disclosure |
| Virtue Ethics | Coordinated disclosure with integrity |
