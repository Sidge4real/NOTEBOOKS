# Virtue Ethics
*NL: Deugdethiek*

> **💡 The core:** Act as a virtuous person would in similar circumstances.

Virtue ethics is an ethical framework that emphasizes the character and virtues of the person performing an action, rather than the action itself or its consequences. The central idea is that moral worth is determined by the agent's intentions and the virtues they embody, such as courage, compassion, and wisdom.

This approach is strongly associated with ancient philosophers like *Aristotle*, who argued that the goal of human life is to achieve eudaimonia (flourishing or well-being) through the cultivation of virtuous character traits.

## Core Idea
Virtue ethics focuses on who we are and how we ought to live.

It asks questions such as:

- What kind of person should I be
- What virtues are most important
- How can I develop a good character

The emphasis is on **moral character** and **the good life**.

## Key Concepts
- **Virtue**: A desirable character trait or quality, such as honesty, kindness, or bravery.
- **Eudaimonia**: The state of flourishing or well-being that results from living a virtuous life.
- **Moral character**: The set of virtues and vices that define a person's ethical nature.
- **Practical wisdom  (phronesis)**: The ability to make good decisions in complex situations based on one's understanding of what is right and good.
