# Introduction
Ethics is a branch of philosophy that deals with questions about what is morally right and wrong, good and bad, fair and unfair. It provides a framework for understanding how we should act and make decisions in various situations, especially when it comes to the impact of our actions on others and the world around us.

## What is ethics?
Ethics is concerned with the principles and values that guide human behavior. It explores questions such as:

- What is the right thing to do in a given situation?
- How should we treat others?
- What are our responsibilities to society and the environment?
- How do we balance our own interests with the interests of others?

Ethics helps us reflect on our choices and understand the moral implications behind them. It is not just about following rules or laws, but about developing a deeper understanding of what it means to live a good and meaningful life, both as individuals and as members of a community.

## Why is ethics important in IT?
In the field of information technology, ethics is essential because technology has a profound and growing impact on individuals, communities, and society as a whole. The decisions we make when designing, deploying, or regulating technology can influence privacy, security, equality, and even the environment.

Ethical thinking helps us navigate these complex challenges and ensures that technology is used responsibly and for the benefit of society. Ethical questions arise in areas such as:

- Data privacy and protection
- Artificial intelligence and automation
- Digital divide and access to technology
- Environmental sustainability of IT infrastructure
- Ethical implications of emerging technologies like biotechnology and nanotechnology

By applying ethical principles, IT professionals can make informed decisions that promote fairness, respect, and responsibility in the digital world.

**Several insights from modern IT ethics highlight why ethical reflection is necessary:**

### Technology is not neutral
Every technological system — from algorithms to interfaces — embeds assumptions, values, and design choices. As *Paul Virilio* famously noted:

> “When you invent the ship, you also invent the shipwreck.”  
New technologies bring new possibilities, but also new risks, harms, and unintended consequences.

### Design choices influence behavior
Recommendation systems, persuasive interfaces, and dark patterns can shape user behavior, often prioritizing engagement over well‑being. These systems can especially harm vulnerable groups and reinforce existing inequalities.

### Data and algorithms carry ethical risks
Modern IT systems rely heavily on data and automation, which introduces risks such as:

- misuse of personal data
- bias in datasets
- structural discrimination
- lack of transparency
- high‑risk autonomous decision‑making

Without ethical oversight, these risks can scale rapidly.

### Regulation alone is not enough
Frameworks like the **GDPR** and the upcoming **AI Act** provide important protections, but they cannot anticipate every ethical dilemma. Technical compliance does not guarantee ethical behavior.

IT professionals must think beyond the law and consider the broader societal impact of their work.

### Developers influence rights and freedoms
Every design decision — from data collection to interface layout — can affect:

- privacy
- autonomy
- fairness
- accessibility
- safety

Ethical thinking helps IT professionals anticipate harm before implementation and design systems that respect human dignity.
