# Consequentialism
*NL: Gevolgsethiek*

> **💡 The core:** Act according to the consequences of your actions.

Consequentialism is an ethical framework that evaluates actions based on their outcomes or consequences. The central idea is that the morality of an action is determined solely by its results, rather than by the intentions behind it or the rules it violates.

This approach is strongly associated with philosophers like *Jeremy Bentham* and *John Stuart Mill*, who argued that the best action is the one that maximizes overall well-being or happiness.

## Core Idea
Consequentialism focuses on what happens as a result of our actions.

It asks questions such as:

- What are the consequences of this action
- Which outcome is most beneficial
- How can I maximize overall well-being

The emphasis is on **the impact of our choices** and **the greatest good for the greatest number**.

## Key Concepts
- **Happiness**: The ultimate goal of consequentialist ethics, often equated with pleasure and the absence of pain.
- **Utility**: A measure of the overall well-being or satisfaction produced by an action.
- **Greatest good for the greatest number**: The principle that an action is right if it promotes the greatest amount of happiness or well-being for the largest number of people.
- **Moral worth**: An action's moral value is determined by its contribution to overall utility.
