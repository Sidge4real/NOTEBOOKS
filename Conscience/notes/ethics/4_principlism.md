# Principlism 
*NL: Beginselenethiek*

> **💡 The core:** Act according to principles that are always right, regardless of the consequences.

Principlism, also known as **deontological ethics**, is an ethical framework that **evaluates actions based on rules, duties, and moral principles, rather than on their consequences**. The central idea is that some actions are morally right or wrong in themselves, regardless of the outcomes they produce.

This approach is strongly associated with the philosopher *Immanuel Kant*, who argued that moral actions must be guided by universal principles that apply to everyone, in every situation.

## Core Idea
Principlism focuses on what we ought to do.

It asks questions such as:

- What rules or duties apply in this situation
- What principles should guide my behavior
- Would it be acceptable if everyone acted this way

The emphasis is on **consistency, fairness, and respect** for persons.

## Key Concepts
- **Duty**: A moral obligation to act in a certain way, regardless of the consequences.
- **Obligation**: A requirement to follow certain rules or principles.
- **Universal principle**: A moral rule that applies to everyone, in every situation.
- **Respect for persons**: Treating individuals as ends in themselves, not merely as means to an end.
- **Moral law**: A set of principles that govern moral behavior, which must be followed regardless of the consequences.
