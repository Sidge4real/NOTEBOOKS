# DigitAll — Digital Inclusion by Design

> Notes from the session on digital inclusion by design, presented by DigitAll at the Digital Impact Day 2024. *(from video resource provided by UC Leuven-Limburg)*

## What is Digital Inclusion?
Digital inclusion means:

- Access to technology
- Skills to use technology
- Opportunities to participate digitally

## The Digital Divide in Belgium
- **40%** of Belgians (16–74) are digitally vulnerable.
- Based on studies by:
  - Koning Boudewijnstichting
  - UCLouvain CIRTES
  - UGent
  - Statbel
  - imec-mict-ugent

## What Does Digital Exclusion Look Like?
- Difficulty using online services.
- Lack of devices or connectivity.
- Low digital literacy.
- Stress or confusion when navigating digital tools.

# Digital Inclusion by Design Index

A framework with **7 criteria** to evaluate and improve digital inclusivity.

## 1. Trust
- Secure and transparent data handling.
- Clear privacy and cookie policies.
- HTTPS support, even for older devices.

## 2. Accessibility
- Technical standards ensuring access for all.
- Inclusive design for people with impairments.

## 3. Interoperability & Compatibility
- Works on any device (old/new, mobile/desktop).
- Consistent experience everywhere.

## 4. Continuous User Involvement
- Involve digitally vulnerable users at every stage.
- Co‑creation and testing with real users.

## 5. Clear Language
- Simple, understandable language.
- Avoid jargon and complexity.

## 6. Transparency
- Show commitment to digital inclusion.
- Communicate openly about design choices.

## 7. User Journey
- Smooth, stress‑free navigation.
- Reduce friction and cognitive load.

# The Index Tool
- Provides a score from:
  - Not Inclusive  
  - Limited Inclusive  
  - Emergent Inclusive  
  - Structured Inclusive  
  - Leading the Way  
- Gives actionable recommendations per criterion.

## Key Takeaways
- Assess needs thoroughly.
- Design flexible, accessible learning paths.
- Engage stakeholders.
- Measure progress continuously.
- Promote lifelong learning.
