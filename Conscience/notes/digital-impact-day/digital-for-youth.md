# Digital for Youth

> Notes from the session on digital inclusion by design, presented by Digital for Youth at the Digital Impact Day 2024. *(from video resource provided by UC Leuven-Limburg)*

## Mission
Digital for Youth strengthens digital inclusion in Belgium by providing refurbished laptops to young people, schools, and youth organisations.

## Problem Context
- **18.2%** of the Belgian population is at risk of poverty or social exclusion.
- **29%** of children with high deprivation use only one or no IT device.
- Only **17%** of e‑waste is collected (expected to double by 2030).
- **49%** of Flemish employees rate their AI skills as “poor”.

## Impact
- **27,500 laptops** delivered since 2019.
- In 2024: **16,000 youngsters reached** (average of 6.8 children per laptop).
- **1,979 laptops** distributed for free via KBS project calls.

## Ecological Impact
- 70% of collected devices are refurbished.
- **8,091 tons CO₂ saved** (equal to 1,734 cars/year).
- **301,559 m²** land use avoided.
- **1,034 kg** conflict minerals recycled.
- **6,326 kg** toxic materials removed.

## New Initiative: AI for Youth
- Hands‑on AI workshops for kids aged 10–15.
- Focus on ethics, deepfakes, ecological impact, and empowerment.
- Co‑creation with children.
- Mobile classroom: **Digitruck**.
- Collaboration with Brightlab (Imec).

## Student Projects (UCLL)
### Hardware Project
- Organise hardware collection (laptops, tablets, smartphones).
- Raise awareness about refurbishment and reuse.
- Visit youth organisations and Circular IT Group.

### AI for Youth Project
- Provide input for Brightlab.
- Develop 30‑minute educational activities.
- Emphasise ethics, energy use, and responsible AI.
- Possible “train the trainer” sessions for children.
