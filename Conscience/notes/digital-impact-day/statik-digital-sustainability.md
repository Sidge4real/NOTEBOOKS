# Statik — Digital Sustainability

> Notes from the session on digital sustainability by Statik at the Digital Impact Day 2024. *(from video resource provided by UC Leuven-Limburg)*

## Core Idea
Digital products have ecological, social, and human impact.  
Statik uses the **5 U’s framework** to guide sustainable digital design.

## The 5 U’s Framework

### 1. Useful
- Start with “Why should this exist?”
- Ensure the product creates real value.
- Align with responsible consumption and production.

### 2. Usable
- Put the audience first.
- “You are not your audience.”
- Don’t skip user testing.
- Ensure accessibility and inclusivity.

### 3. Used
- A product that is not used is not sustainable.
- Communicate effectively.
- Focus on the right reach, not the widest reach.
- Dare to delete unnecessary communication.

### 4. Resources Used
- Measure and understand digital carbon footprint.
- Factors:
  - Data transfer
  - Energy intensity
  - Hosting energy source
  - Carbon intensity of electricity
  - Traffic volume

#### Practical Guidelines
- Clean code.
- Compress images.
- Avoid video unless necessary.
- Choose colours wisely.
- Avoid custom fonts.
- Limit animations.
- Use caching for returning visitors.
- Choose green hosting.

### 5. Don’t Abuse
- Treat users ethically.
- Avoid dark patterns.
- Choose insights over excessive data.
- Personalise only when relevant.
- Respect privacy (GDPR, transparent cookies).

## Examples
- HLN.be: **0.54g CO₂ per pageview** → huge impact at scale.
- 2dehands.be: **0.41g CO₂/view**
- Nieuwsblad.be: **1.20g CO₂/view**

## Key Takeaways
- Make intended impact concrete.
- Prioritise accessibility and inclusivity.
- Improve based on user feedback.
- Spend resources wisely.
- Treat users the way you want to be treated.
