# Digital Inclusion

## Digital vulnerability in Belgium
- 40% of Belgians between 16 and 74 are digitally vulnerable.
- 5% do not use the internet at all.
- 35% have weak basic digital skills.
- Belgium scores 1% worse than the European average.

## What digital vulnerability means
Digital vulnerability occurs when people:

- lack access to technology,
- lack the skills to use technology,
- or lack opportunities to participate digitally.

This limits participation in education, employment, administration, healthcare, and social life.

## Why this matters for IT professionals
As an IT professional, you design the systems these people try to use every day.

Your design choices determine:

- whether someone can complete a form,
- whether someone can book an appointment,
- whether someone can apply for a job,
- whether someone can participate in the digital society at all.

Digital inclusion is not optional; it is a core responsibility.

## What you can do
- Design for everyone, not just digital experts.
- Keep interfaces simple, clear, and accessible.
- Use understandable language.
- Test with real users, including those with low digital skills.
- Ensure your product works on older devices and slow connections.
- Avoid digital barriers such as mandatory accounts, complex flows, or unclear error messages.

## Key message
Digital inclusion is about access, skills, and opportunities.  
A digital product is only successful when everyone can use it.
